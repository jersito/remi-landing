The Problem

When everyone tracks differently, nobody has the full picture

Most baby apps assume one person does most of the tracking. One account. One phone. The "share with partner" feature is usually an afterthought, or just awkward to use.

Real life doesn't work like that. Your partner does the 3AM feed. Your mom comes over Saturday afternoon. The nanny has baby from 9 to 5. Everyone has different information. Nobody has the full picture.

Sound familiar?

"Did she eat today?" "I don't know, ask your mom." "When did he last nap?" "The nanny didn't write it down." Notes in three places. Texts you screenshot. A paper log on the counter two days out of date. A pediatrician appointment where you're trying to remember the last 48 hours from memory.

Why voice logging matters for shared accounts

When multiple people are logging, speed matters even more. Your partner might not remember the exact time. Your nanny might be holding a fussy baby. Your mom might not be comfortable tapping through menus.

Saying "he just had 5oz" works for everyone. No training needed. No "how do I change the feed type" text to you in the middle of your workday. The lower the friction for every caregiver, the more complete the timeline.

FAQ

Common questions

Is family sharing really free?
Yes. Sharing the timeline with your partner, nanny, and grandparents is in the free tier. You don't need Pippy Premium for this.
How many caregivers can I invite?
Everyone who helps care for your baby. Partner, nanny, grandparents, babysitter, and so on.
Can daycare log for me?
If your provider is willing to use the app, yes. If not, you can manually log whatever they wrote on the daily report when you pick up baby. Everything lives in one place either way.
Does everyone see everything I log?
Everyone you invite sees the shared timeline. If you need more fine-grained permissions, let us know at our contact page and we'll flag demand.
