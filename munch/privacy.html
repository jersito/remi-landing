Munch Privacy Policy

Everything you swipe stays on your phone.

Last updated: April 25, 2026

Short version: Munch has no servers, no accounts, and no analytics. Your swipes live only on your device. Couples mode runs peer-to-peer over your local Wi-Fi — nothing leaves the network. Uninstall the app and it's gone.

Who this policy covers

This policy covers the mobile app Munch ("the app"). The app is part of the Pippy family, operated by the same team behind the Pippy baby tracker at meetpippy.com. The Pippy baby tracker has its own privacy policy at meetpippy.com/privacy.html.

What the app collects

Nothing that leaves your device. The app saves the following on your phone using the operating system's local storage:

  • Your per-cuisine swipe history (yes / no counts) so the next round can surface what you've loved before
  • Your display name (only used so your partner can see who they're matched with during a session)
  • Round count, used to rotate the dish photo shown for each cuisine

That's the complete list. No email, no account, no device identifier, no location, no contacts, no photos.

What we don't do

  • We don't run servers that receive your swipes or matches.
  • We don't use analytics, crash reporting, or telemetry of any kind.
  • We don't show ads.
  • We don't track you across apps or websites.
  • We don't sell, share, or rent your data, because we don't have it.

Couples mode (live, on the same Wi-Fi)

When you and a partner open the share screen on the same local network, both phones advertise themselves on the network so you can see each other and connect directly. The connection is a peer-to-peer TCP socket between your two phones — no server in the middle. The data exchanged is the names you each chose, plus the cuisine IDs you've each said yes to. The connection closes when either of you closes the app or leaves the screen. None of this traffic leaves your local Wi-Fi network.

Snapshot share (deep link)

If you want to send your cravings to a partner who's not on the same Wi-Fi, the app builds a short deep link containing a list of your liked cuisine IDs and your name, and hands it to your phone's share sheet. When you pick Messages, WhatsApp, or email, your phone sends it through that app. We never see it.

Camera

The app uses your camera only to scan a QR code that contains coordinates for the live couples session (an IP address and port on your local network, plus a one-time room code). The camera feed is not recorded, not stored, and not transmitted. The camera permission is requested only when you open the QR scanner.

Local Wi-Fi network access

iOS asks your permission the first time the app uses the local network. The app uses local network access only for the live couples mode described above (mDNS service discovery and a direct phone-to-phone TCP connection). It does not connect to any other devices on your network or send anything outside it.

App updates

The app is built with the Expo framework. When it launches, it quietly checks for over-the-air app updates by making an HTTPS request to Expo's update server at u.expo.dev. That request contains only standard information every HTTPS request contains: your device's IP address, the app's version, your platform (iOS), and the project identifier. No swipes, names, or anything you've logged is sent. Expo's privacy policy is at expo.dev/privacy.

Your local data

You can clear all your swipe history from within the app in Settings. Uninstalling the app also removes the local data. We cannot restore data from an uninstalled app, because we never had a copy.

Children

The app is designed for adults choosing what to eat. It is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from anyone, including children, because we do not collect information at all.

Changes to this policy

If we ever start collecting something (we don't plan to), we'll update this page and announce it in the app before the change takes effect.
