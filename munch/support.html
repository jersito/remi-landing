Common questions

My partner's phone isn't showing up in couples mode.

Both phones need to be on the same Wi-Fi network. Some networks (corporate, hotel, public) block phones from seeing each other — home Wi-Fi works fine. If discovery still doesn't work, use the QR fallback on the share screen.

The match splash didn't pop when we both said yes.

Make sure both phones show "Connected" in the banner. If iOS suspended one phone in the background for too long, the connection may have dropped silently — reopen the app and start a fresh session.

Why does it ask for camera and local network permission?

The camera is used only to scan your partner's QR code (the off-network fallback). Local network access is needed to find your partner's phone on the same Wi-Fi for live couples mode. Both are explained on the prompts and in our privacy policy.

Why did my swipe history disappear?

Munch stores everything locally on your phone. Uninstalling the app, clearing app data, or moving to a new device removes the history, since we don't have a cloud copy. This is by design — it's what keeps the app free and private.

How do I delete my data?

In the app: open Settings and tap "Reset history." Or delete the app. Either removes everything. We've never had a copy.

Does Munch work without internet?

Yes — fully. The app has zero internet requirements after install. Live couples mode runs over your local Wi-Fi, not the internet, so it works in airplane mode as long as Wi-Fi is on.

Can I match with someone in another city?

Not live, but you can send them a snapshot of your cravings as a deep link from the share screen. They open the link and see a comparison of where the two of you overlap.
