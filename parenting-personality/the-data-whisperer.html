The Data Whisperer

You don't just track, you understand. Your data tells a story most parents never think to read.

The archetype

A cartographer of a tiny, chaotic country.

You can see patterns three weeks before anyone else can.

You notice the shifts before the forum posts start. You clock the feed changes before anyone else does. You are running a quiet, continuous investigation and your subject adores you for it.

Your love language is noticing.

You remember the one feed where they took an extra ounce. You remember the Tuesday they skipped a nap. To you, tracking is not cold, it is the opposite. It is saying I see you, every day, even the ordinary ones.

When it gets hard, you gather evidence.

While other parents spiral, you open the app. You look for the line in the data that says "this is temporary" or "this is the real thing". You are the calm in the room because you did the reading.

The flaw that is not

People call you intense. Your pediatrician calls you prepared.

Your visit notes make doctors sit up straighter. The parents who complain about you are the ones winging it. You are not overthinking. You are taking your baby seriously in a world that treats infancy like a mystery.

Your parent twin

Your parent energy: the quiet investigator.

Part detective, part archivist, part devoted apprentice. You will know this baby better than any medical chart ever could.

How Pippy assigns this type

You get this type when your logs are rich with detail. Measured volumes, careful notes, rarely a blank field. Pippy recognizes the pattern of a parent who tracks with intention.

Pippy does not ask you to fill out a quiz. Your parenting personality comes from the way you already log feeds, sleep, diapers, and milestones. After a few days of logging, your type appears in the Fun tab of the app, with a shareable card.

A closing note

Your future self is going to read this log one day and it will take their breath away.
