The All-In Parent

The boring stuff. The big stuff. Everything in between. You don't do halfway, and your baby knows it.

The archetype

A tiny human's full-time everything.

You did not choose a lane because your baby did not.

Babies come at you in every direction at once. Hunger, sleep, skin, mood, growth, noise. Most parents pick the two they can manage and let the rest blur. You refused to blur any of it. You see your child in full color.

Your love language is relentlessness.

Not the burnt-out kind. The warm, unwavering kind. You show up for the boring parts with the same attention as the big parts. Your baby is not just being parented, they are being witnessed, all the way through.

When it gets hard, you just keep going.

You do not negotiate with the hard moments. You do not outsource the messy parts. The long night, the fussy feed, the stretch where nothing seems to work. You handle it the same way you handle everything: head down, heart open.

The flaw that is not

People warn you about burning out. They have not met you.

The thing about being all-in is that you get it back tenfold. The connection you are building is not fragile. It is the kind your kid will describe in therapy one day, but in a good way.

Your parent twin

Your parent energy: Bluey's Bandit.

Present for every game. Down for every bit. Not too cool to kneel on the floor. The kind of parent kids write books about when they grow up.

How Pippy assigns this type

You get this type when you log widely and often. Pippy sees a parent showing up across every corner of baby life.

Pippy does not ask you to fill out a quiz. Your parenting personality comes from the way you already log feeds, sleep, diapers, and milestones. After a few days of logging, your type appears in the Fun tab of the app, with a shareable card.

A closing note

You are not doing too much. You are doing exactly what this stage deserves, and your baby knows it.
