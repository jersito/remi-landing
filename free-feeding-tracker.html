Reassurance

Signs your baby is getting enough

The tracker shows the numbers. Your baby shows you the truth. If most of these are lining up, intake is almost always fine.

💧

Wet diapers

After day 5, aim for 6 or more heavy wet diapers in 24 hours. Pale yellow urine is what you want to see.

💩

Regular poops

At least 3 to 4 stools a day in the first weeks. After 6 weeks, breastfed babies can skip days and still be healthy.

📈

Weight gain

Most babies regain birth weight by two weeks, then gain about 5 to 7 oz per week in the early months.

😌

Content after feeds

Hands relax, body softens, and baby comes off the breast or bottle on their own. Not every feed looks like this, but most should.

👂

Swallowing you can hear

On the breast, you hear small gulps and see a jaw-drop pattern. On the bottle, you see steady swallows, not frantic gulping.

🌱

Steady growth

Growth curves trending in their own lane at checkups. Big babies stay big, small babies stay small. Both are fine.

Guide

How to use this feeding tracker

A feeding log is one of the most useful tools in the newborn months. It tells you when the last feed happened when your brain is too tired to remember, spots cluster feeding nights before they feel random, and gives your pediatrician real numbers instead of guesses. This tracker stores every entry in your browser, so it works offline after the page loads and nothing leaves your device.

What to log for breastfeeding

For a nursing session tracker, the two most useful data points are the time you started and the minutes on each side. Use the "breast left", "breast right", or "both sides" options and enter the total minutes. A typical newborn feed is 20 to 45 minutes. As your baby gets older and more efficient, feeds often shrink to 10 to 20 minutes and that is totally normal.

What to log for bottle feeds

For an online bottle tracker, log the oz or ml offered and how much was actually taken. Use the note field for leftovers, spit-up, or a bottle refusal. If you are mixing breast milk and formula, pick "bottle" for breast milk in a bottle and "formula" for formula. Pumped milk feeds can be logged separately if you want to see your pumping output versus your baby's intake.

Breast vs bottle: should you track differently?

Breastfed babies do not need ounce-level accuracy. Watching diapers, weight gain, and general mood matters more. But logging timing helps you see if cluster feeding is normal for your baby, when the longest stretch usually falls, and whether one side is consistently empty faster than the other. Bottle-fed babies are easier to track by volume, so the ounce total becomes your daily anchor number.

Cluster feeding, explained

Cluster feeding is when your baby wants to eat back-to-back for several hours, usually in the evening, often before a longer night stretch. It is most intense around 2 to 3 weeks, 6 weeks, and 3 months, and lines up with growth spurts. If you are logging every 30 to 60 minutes for 3 or 4 hours and then seeing a longer stretch of sleep, that is a classic cluster pattern. It is exhausting but normal.

When to contact your pediatrician

  • Fewer than 6 wet diapers in 24 hours after the first week.
  • Baby has not regained birth weight by two weeks old.
  • You cannot wake your baby for a feed, or baby is sleeping through every feed for more than 4 to 5 hours in a row in the first month.
  • Signs of dehydration: sunken soft spot, no tears when crying, very dark urine, dry mouth.
  • A sudden, lasting drop in feed volume or duration that is not explained by a growth spurt or teething.
  • You are worried. You know your baby. Call.

Privacy

Your feeds are saved in your browser's localStorage under the key pippy_feeds_v1. They never touch a server and we cannot see them. Clearing your browser data or using private mode will wipe the log, so use the Download PDF button before switching devices or clearing history.
