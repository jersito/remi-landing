Why most baby apps feel "mom-coded"

Open any popular baby app and you'll notice the same patterns. Soft pink palettes. Breastfeeding as the default feed type. "Mama" in the copy. The assumption that one primary caregiver (usually mom) is the one opening the app.

Dads use these apps too. You log the 2AM bottle. You change the diaper while she gets 20 more minutes of sleep. You take baby to the pediatrician. The app just never seems to notice you're there.

Pippy doesn't try to be trendy about it. It just defaults to neutral. Feed type is whatever you're feeding. The timeline is shared between whoever is caring for baby. You log in your voice.

FAQ

Is Pippy good for dads specifically?
Pippy is designed to be gender-neutral from the ground up. No "mama" copy, no defaults that assume breastfeeding, shared timelines as the default. Dads, partners, nannies, and grandparents all use the same interface.
Can I share with my partner for free?
Yes. Family sharing is in the free tier. Everyone you invite sees and logs to the same timeline in real time.
Does it work at 3AM with one hand?
That's the main design goal. You say "he had 4oz" or "changed a wet diaper" and Pippy logs it. No tapping through menus while holding a baby.
What about breastfeeding vs bottle?
Pippy handles both. Say "she nursed for 15 minutes on the left" or "he had 3oz from a bottle" and it logs accurately. No forced default.
